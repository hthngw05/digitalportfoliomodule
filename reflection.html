05 — reflection

What I am
becoming.

The internship is still in progress. These reflections capture how my understanding of a Data Analyst’s work has developed so far.

01

Nature of work

I previously associated data analysis mainly with cleaning datasets, calculating metrics and creating visualisations. I now understand that an industrial Data Analyst must first study the process that produces the data. My work involves learning manufacturing terminology, speaking with stakeholders, testing interpretations and documenting limitations before presenting a result.

02

Challenges faced

My main challenge has been uncertainty: incomplete MRP fields, inconsistent traveller content and insufficient labelled X-ray images. I learned not to hide uncertainty behind assumptions. Instead, I identify what is confirmed, test possible approaches, ask for clarification and communicate what the output cannot yet support.

03

Learning and growth

I have broadened my knowledge beyond classroom analytics into MRP, MES, Odoo and manufacturing traceability. I have also become more comfortable changing an approach when evidence shows that it is unreliable. Choosing Stamp ID over operator-name OCR taught me that a simpler solution can produce greater operational value.

04

Accomplishments

My key accomplishment so far is connecting several workstreams to one digitalisation objective: improving how production information is captured, interpreted and used. Although the projects remain at different stages, I can now explain their business purpose, technical constraints and responsible next steps more clearly.

05

My future work self

I want to grow into an AI researcher who investigates how technology can be useful to the world. This experience is teaching me that useful AI must be grounded in real needs, good data, domain knowledge and ethical judgment. I hope to contribute to AI systems that support people responsibly rather than creating complexity without purpose.
