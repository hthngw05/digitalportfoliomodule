The opportunity

X-ray inspection can reveal internal PCB assembly conditions that are not visible from the surface. AI assistance may eventually help inspectors locate suspicious regions more consistently and focus their attention.

The key constraint

There was no suitable labelled defect dataset available. Without representative images, agreed defect classes and expert annotations, model performance could not be evaluated responsibly.

My contribution

  • Researched the existing X-ray inspection context.
  • Considered how an AI layer could complement the current machine.
  • Identified data availability as the principal feasibility constraint.
  • Outlined the need for expert annotation and validation criteria.
  • Used the findings to assess a possible Major Project direction.

Responsible use

An industrial model should support—not silently replace—qualified inspection decisions. False negatives, data drift and explainability must be considered alongside accuracy.

Research
insight

I began by thinking about model selection, but the research shifted my attention to data readiness. In industrial AI, a sophisticated model cannot compensate for unclear labels or unrepresentative data. The more responsible starting point is to define the data, expert review process and acceptable risk.
