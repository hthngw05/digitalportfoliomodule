Making MRP
easier to read.

RoleData analysis & visualisation
ToolsExcel, SAP data, Tableau planning
OJT connectionDashboard creation

The context

Material Requirements Planning data contains dated demand, supply, stock and procurement elements. When these records are presented vertically across many materials, planners may need considerable effort to trace what is required on each date and determine the next action.

The challenge

The dataset contained missing values, multiple MRP element types and business fields whose meanings could not be safely inferred from their labels. The available extract also did not contain every value required for a complete planning calculation.

My contribution

  • Explored 7,550 records covering 420 materials.
  • Identified eight MRP element types and parent-component relationships.
  • Restructured dated records into a horizontal overview.
  • Clarified that the Date field represented the required date.
  • Considered future Tableau visualisation, action identification and automation.

Use of the output

The proposed structure provides a clearer foundation for reviewing demand by date. It can later support planning dashboards and automated explanations once the business rules and source fields have been fully validated.

What changed
in my thinking?

I learned that reliable analytics depends on business definitions and data lineage, not only formulas. When a field was unclear, I separated confirmed meanings from assumptions and asked for clarification. A visually complete dashboard would be misleading if the underlying planning logic had not been verified.
