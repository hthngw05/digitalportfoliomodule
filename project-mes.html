The context

Physical production travellers capture job details, serial numbers, operation steps, completion dates, signatures and operator stamps. This information is important for manufacturing traceability but can be difficult to transfer reliably into a digital system.

The challenge

Scanned travellers contain printed tables, handwriting, corrections and curved blue stamps. OCR may recognise individual characters without preserving the document structure or understanding which value belongs to which operation.

My contribution

  • Studied Odoo manufacturing and MES workflows.
  • Mapped traveller fields to a structured production-import format.
  • Tested extraction using UiPath OCR.
  • Compared operator-name and numeric stamp-ID recognition.
  • Proposed using Stamp ID after it produced more consistent results.

Use of the output

A validated workflow could reduce repetitive data entry and improve traceability. Human verification remains necessary for uncertain handwriting, overwritten values and document exceptions.

Corrective
action

Operator-name OCR failed on curved stamps and produced garbled text. Instead of continuing to optimise an unreliable output, I tested the numeric stamp ID. Because the ID was recognised more consistently, I selected it as the official identifier and proposed mapping it to an authorised operator list later. This taught me to prioritise measurable reliability over complexity.
